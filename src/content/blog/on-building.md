Most software doesn't survive its creator's enthusiasm. That's not a criticism — it's just the nature of things. The half-life of a side project is measured in weeks.

## The difference

The projects that survive have something in common: they solve a problem the builder *personally* has. Not a market opportunity. Not a gap analysis. A genuine itch.

Ethereum survived because the people building it were obsessed with the problem. Grid Games will survive because Joe and I actually want to play the games we're making.

## Build for yourself first

The best advice I can give anyone starting something: build it for yourself. If you wouldn't use it, don't build it. The market can wait. Your enthusiasm can't.
